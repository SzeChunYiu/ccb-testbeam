## Cross-theme Verdict

The program is converging on a clear rule: ML is strongest when the target is independently defined and the signal is in waveform shape, especially duplicate-readout charge, saturation-like recovery, anomaly isolation, and injected two-pulse RMS. It is weak or only diagnostic when a strong analytic comparator already exists, or when the target is derived from the same timing/shape observable. The largest physics-facing open gaps are not more generic ML models; they are missing truth handles and support calibration.
